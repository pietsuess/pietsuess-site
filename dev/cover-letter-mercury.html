Creative Director & Post-Production Veteran
Salt Spring Island, BC  |  pietsuess.com

What I love about Mercury as a product is how it's rebuilding a stodgy old institution into a perfectly tuned sports car. Banking has always been a clunky behemoth. A big ship that's hard to steer — and it feels like it from the inside. Mercury handles. Quicksilver, like the name. That's the kind of problem I've spent my whole career solving — across every medium, format, and scale that the work has required of me.

I love the design, the feel, and the graphics on the Mercury website. It's beautiful, it's sexy, and it draws me into the information while keeping me engaged. You clearly have a talented team already, and those graphics are just begging to be animated. Has that work begun?

In this current industrial revolution, the thing that will break through the noise — as it always has — is the product of taste. These new tools are force multipliers. Not magic wands. One person can now do what used to take a team. I've been cracking open tools since I was a kid — painting pixels in MS-DOS Paintbrush, editing the source code of Gorillas and Nibbles to make them do things they weren't supposed to. That instinct hasn't changed, rather has grown with skill and experience. I'm always the first one in — first to learn the tool, first to break it, first to find what it's actually good for.

Because knowing what's right, what's almost right, and what needs to be thrown away — that's not a prompt. That's over thirty-five years of refining every frame, every cut, every colour choice until it's earned. People trust my eye and my opinion because I've spent decades sharpening both.

I'm willing to handle any part of the work being created. At the Salt Spring Center of Yoga, I managed an entire business, brand, and community — operations, identity, and culture all running at once — including their annual 500+ person summer retreat with a team of fifty. Social campaigns, print media, merchandise, all of it. And I do it with ease and a playful spirit. I've filmed in remote parts of the world and embedded in communities to give projects a depth they wouldn't have had otherwise.

The thing I'm best at is leading teams. I find people's greatest strengths and push them to go further. I work hard, I enjoy the work, and that energy is contagious — people want to be part of it. Working with Maximum Effort, I learned that I'm capable of operating at the highest level and still teaching people new abilities — even if I've only just learned them myself. If speed is required, I find the fastest path. I'm a problem solver. And I love the puzzle and the pressure. I directed Ben Affleck in an animation for his Eastern Congo Initiative — a film he tweeted “Here's another film I'm proud of” on Oscar night, moments before Argo won Best Picture. I've spent 30+ years leading creative work in every discipline the job has put in front of me.

Mercury's brand already has something rare. Taste and smooth handling. I want to be there innovating into the future alongside it.

I'm a US citizen based in British Columbia, experienced working remotely across time zones.
